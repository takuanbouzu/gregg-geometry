1 · The Observation

One mathematical structure, expressed three ways

Gregg Fleishman's work — the slot joint, the √2 system, the rhombic dodecahedron, the FCC lattice — can be formally understood through the lens of dual polyhedra theory and specifically the Dorman Luke construction. This is not a loose analogy; the mathematical structure maps precisely onto Gregg's physical and architectural practice.

The √2 system, the rhombic dodecahedron, and the FCC lattice are not three separate interests. They are the same mathematical fact expressed in three different registers.

2 · The Construction

What the Dorman Luke construction is

A geometric method for deriving the face shape of a dual polyhedron from the vertex figure of its primal. Formalized by Dorman Luke and detailed in Magnus Wenninger's Dual Models (Cambridge University Press, 1983).

  1. At vertex V of a uniform polyhedron, mark points A, B, C, D — the midpoints of each edge meeting at V, equidistant from V.
  2. Connect A, B, C, D to form the vertex figure ABCD.
  3. Draw the circumcircle of ABCD.
  4. Draw the tangent line to the circumcircle at each corner (A, B, C, D).
  5. Mark points E, F, G, H where adjacent tangent lines intersect. The polygon EFGH is the face of the dual polyhedron.

The critical condition: the midsphere

The construction only works when the polyhedron has a midsphere (intersphere) — a sphere tangent to every edge at its midpoint, shared by both the primal and its dual. For uniform polyhedra, this condition is always met.

The midsphere is the geometric bridge between a structure and its dual. Every edge of both the primal and dual is tangent to the same sphere.

The canonical example

Wikipedia's illustration uses the cuboctahedron → rhombic dodecahedron. This is not incidental to Gregg's work — it is the exact transformation at the center of his geometric system.

  • The cuboctahedron (vector equilibrium) is the vertex-transitive primal; its vertex figures are squares
  • The Dorman Luke construction applied to those square vertex figures produces rhombic faces with diagonal ratio √2 : 1
  • The resulting dual is the rhombic dodecahedron — also the Voronoi cell of the FCC lattice
  • This is why the √2 proportioning appears throughout Gregg's system: it is a direct consequence of the duality geometry
3 · In Architectural Terms

The slot joint as vertex figure

The geometry of connection — vertex figure and circumcircle tangency — fully determines the geometry of the resulting face, and therefore the geometry of the dual structure.

In Gregg's physical work, the slot joint is the vertex figure. How pieces meet at a node — the angle, the slot width, the √2 proportioning — determines the planar geometry of each structural panel or face, which in turn determines the space-filling behavior of the whole system.

Gregg is not just working with rhombic dodecahedra. He is physically instantiating the process by which the rhombic dodecahedron is generated — building the Dorman Luke construction in plywood and aluminum at furniture and architectural scale.

4 · The Lineage

Thinkers who circled the same territory

Every thinker below arrived at the vertex-figure-determines-structure principle. None named the Dorman Luke construction or the mathematical theory of dual uniform polyhedra as the underlying principle they were enacting.

1917

D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson — On Growth and Form

The deep background. Thompson's argument — that biological forms arise from physical and geometric forces, not just evolution — established the paradigm within which Pearce, Fuller, and implicitly Gregg all work. His analysis of cell packing, soap bubble geometry, and skeletal structures is an early description of Voronoi tessellation and minimal-surface duality, predating the formal mathematics.

1961

Konrad Wachsmann — The Turning Point of Building

The universal joint as structural vertex figure. Wachsmann spent decades searching for the "universal knot" — a single connector geometry that could generate any space frame. His Mobilar Structure system (1944–45) for USAF aircraft hangars used one standardized tetrahedral connector for infinite structural extension. He understood that the node is the structure. His search is the architectural version of the Dorman Luke question: what vertex configuration generates the most powerful dual?

1975

Buckminster Fuller — Synergetics and the Vector Equilibrium

Fuller's "vector equilibrium" is the cuboctahedron — the primal polyhedron whose dual, via Dorman Luke, is the rhombic dodecahedron. He understood this as the "zero state" of spatial structure from which all other forms derive. Geodesic domes are physically dual polyhedra: geodesic polyhedra are the duals of Goldberg polyhedra. Like Pearce and Wachsmann, Fuller arrived at the duality principle physically and intuitively without naming it in mathematical terms.

1978

Peter Pearce — Structure in Nature is a Strategy for Design

The closest direct precursor. Pearce worked with Eames and Fuller and founded Synestructics to produce physical kits embodying space-filling polyhedral systems. He argued that joint geometry determines structural typology — the same logic as polyhedral duality — and explicitly worked with the rhombic dodecahedron and FCC tessellation. He arrived there through structural intuition and biological analogy, not formal mathematical derivation. Notably absent: he does not cite Dorman Luke or dual polyhedra theory despite working in exactly that territory.

2020–

Contemporary Computational Research

Recent work has formally connected Voronoi decomposition to interlocking structural assemblies. Yadav et al. (2020) generate interlocking vault modules by decomposing 3D space via Voronoi cells — the interface geometry between modules is the shared face of adjacent Voronoi cells, which determines structural interlocking. This is exactly the dual-face-from-vertex-figure relationship. Active topological interlocking assembly research (2020s) directly formalizes the Dorman Luke principle at architectural scale.

5 · The Gap

What no one has explicitly named

Every thinker in this lineage arrived at the vertex-figure-determines-structure principle through structural intuition, biological analogy, or computational discovery. None appear to have formally named the Dorman Luke construction or the mathematical theory of dual uniform polyhedra as the underlying principle.

Gregg's system encodes the Dorman Luke relationship physically: the slot joint is the vertex figure; the rhombic face is what the construction produces; the √2 proportioning is a direct consequence of that transformation's geometry; no-fastener construction emerges from the same face-interface interlocking logic that topological interlocking research formalizes computationally.

He is working at the intersection of the Pearce / Fuller / Wachsmann design-science tradition and the formal mathematical theory of polyhedral duality — but arrived there through independent material and geometric research, not through either tradition directly.

The Dorman Luke construction is the missing theoretical frame that formally describes what his slot joint system is doing.
