Rhombic Dodecahedron

12 congruent rhombic faces · dual of the cuboctahedron · tiles space perfectly. Each face has diagonal ratio 1 : √2 — the same proportion as the unit cube’s face diagonal.

Construction: begin with a unit cube. Raise a square pyramid over each of its 6 faces. The 12 rhombic faces are where adjacent pyramid faces meet — one face per cube edge.

Face diagonals: each rhombus has a short diagonal = 1 (cube corner to cube corner) and a long diagonal = √2 (pole to pole). Ratio: 1 : √2.
Space-filling: the rhombic dodecahedron tiles 3D space without gaps — key to Gregg’s modular and interlocking structural systems.

Dual: it is the dual of the cuboctahedron. Its 14 vertices correspond to the 14 faces of the cuboctahedron (8 triangles + 6 squares).
