01 — Cube diagonals

Three lengths hidden in a cube

Edge, face diagonal, space diagonal — the proportions 1 : √2 : √3. The single right triangle they form is the Lost Triangle, sitting at 35.26° inside the cube.

Twelve rhombi that tile space

The Truncated Rhombicube

The Fleishman Sequence

The spiral of square roots, stood up in space

Begin with a unit right triangle. Its hypotenuse is √2. Raise a unit leg and the new hypotenuse is √3 — and so on, winding outward forever.

The Lost Triangle is the first turn of a sequence. Carried into three dimensions, the same root-by-root construction rises into space — the Lost Triangle stands up.

Research

One structure, expressed three ways

Gregg's work — the slot joint, the √2 system, the rhombic dodecahedron, the FCC lattice — can be formally understood through dual-polyhedra theory and the Dorman Luke construction. Not a loose analogy: the mathematics maps precisely onto the physical practice.

It situates the work in a lineage reaching from D'Arcy Thompson through Fuller, Pearce, and Wachsmann to contemporary computational research.
