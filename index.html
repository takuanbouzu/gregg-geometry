Unit Cube Diagonal Geometry

The sketch explores three fundamental lengths inside a unit cube (edge = 1):

edge = 1 | face diagonal = sqrt(2) | space diagonal = sqrt(3)

The green triangle shows the key structural relationship: a right triangle with legs 1 and sqrt(2), hypotenuse sqrt(3).

1^2 + (sqrt(2))^2 = 1 + 2 = 3 = (sqrt(3))^2

This produces the characteristic angles: arctan(1/sqrt(2)) approx 35.26 degrees, complement approx 54.74 degrees.

These proportions form the geometric DNA of modular, interlocking structures.
